WWF

Campaign rewarded « PRIX D’OR » at « CHALLENGE DE LA PUB » 2019

Brief

To make a brand content campaign for 14 months (from June 2019 to September 2020 to sensitize the French population on the decline of biodiversity).
WWF France

Observations

Today we are all drown of alarmist messages about environment state through the media. Furthermore, shocked visual supports generate pessimism and restrict people believing in the cause for which they fight. Thus, to act they need optimism.

Campaign goals

1
Informing the public on the actions of the WWF
2
Encourage people to act by giving sense to commitment
3
Encourage people to continue their efforts
Instead of using shocking visual supports, why not exploiting emotions generated by hearing ?
« If a picture is worth a thousand words, a sound environment is worth a thousand images »

Creative inspiration

According to his plentiful studies, he discovers that each sound environment produces its own acoustic signature. The sound of each environment is therefore an indicator of the state of health of the ecosystem concerned.

50% of the sounds recorded since the 1960s have disappeared from the Earth’s surface.

Through his observations, Bernie Krause teaches us to listen to nature, thus confirming his motto:

Campaign concept

« Redonnons sa voix à la nature »

Give nature its voice back

Nature speaks to us. It is abandoned. Let us take back its voice

Today, nature loses its voice. We have to give it back by acting together. To do that, we suggest to unite our voices in order to give meaning to our actions. It is through our shared engagement that we can make a difference more quickly and effectively. Uniting our voices means coordinating our actions. We, therefore, want to focus our campaign on an optimistic vision based on sound in order to make the consumer understand that it is not too late to act. .

Time 1

« Let’s pay attention : let’s keep our ears open »
OBJECTIVE : INFORM AND SENSITIZE

Launch of the campaign Manifesto film

Our film highlights our desire to show the “new sounds” of nature, that is to say the noise nuisances caused by human activity, that overtake the sounds on natural ecosystems and harm wild biodiversity.

This concept creatively translates into a particular contrast. The visual is composed of images of good natural ecosystems, but the discordant sound disturbs the senses of the viewer. Thus, we wish to show that nature loses its voice in the face of human activity.

However, to keep an optimistic tone, we will show at the end of the film that it is not too late to react, and that by uniting, we can re-give nature its voice.

CALL TO ACTION: encourage people to take action and support WWF in its actions and recruit them to the #Wildvoices stream
